## 1) Client identity

- Use **Puriva** as the client brand name.
- Use `puriva.id` as the working domain.
- Treat the client record as one domain-based client hub.
- Do not invent legal entity names, license numbers, or ownership details.

**Safe default when unknown:** use `Puriva` / `the clinic` / `the team`.

## 2) Clinic profile

- Treat the clinic profile as **verification-required** before public use.
- Working profile: Indonesia/Bali-based clinic brand serving local and international patients.
- Verify clinic address, service location, doctor/staff names, and legal entity before any external claim.
- Do not imply medical credentials, facility scale, or partnership status without evidence.

**Safe default when unknown:** say `the clinic` and keep the description generic.

## 3) Brand voice

- Calm, reassuring, premium, clear, and medically careful.
- Avoid hype, fear, urgency, miracle language, and sales pressure.
- Use simple sentences and outcome-neutral wording.
- Keep the voice educational, not promotional.

**Do not use:** `guaranteed`, `instant`, `cure`, `perfect`, `best`, `no risk`, `works for everyone`.

## 4) Languages / service areas

- Default languages: **Indonesian and English**.
- Add other languages only if the client explicitly approves them.
- Service area wording should stay broad unless verified: Bali, Indonesia, and international inquiry support.
- If a region is not confirmed, do not list it as a service area.

**Safe default when unknown:** `available for Indonesian and English-speaking inquiries`.

## 5) Contact / booking facts

Record only verified facts:

- phone / WhatsApp
- email
- address
- booking URL
- consultation hours
- booking method

Never publish guessed contact details.

**Safe default when unknown:** `contact the clinic team for booking details`.

## 6) Services

Current working service clusters:

1. Wegovy / semaglutide weight-management education
2. Stem cell therapy
3. General aesthetic services
4. Bali medical-tourism / international client journey

Use service descriptions as **education and consultation support**, not as outcome promises.

## 7) Wegovy / semaglutide constraints

- Treat as a **prescription-medication / medical-consultation** topic.
- Keep copy educational and eligibility-based.
- Do not claim a specific amount of weight loss.
- Do not claim it is suitable for everyone.
- Do not imply permanent or guaranteed results.
- Do not present medication use as simple lifestyle advice.

**Safe wording:** `consultation-based weight-management support` / `eligibility is assessed by the clinician`.

## 8) Stem cell therapy constraints

- Treat as **verification-required** and medically sensitive.
- Do not claim cure, reversal, regeneration guarantees, or permanent results.
- Do not state partner, lab, or hospital authority unless current evidence exists.
- Keep copy descriptive and cautious.

**Safe wording:** `information about a specialist treatment option` / `suitability is determined after review`.

## 9) Hospital / partner wording

- Use partner wording only with current, documented proof.
- If proof is missing, say `may coordinate with` or omit the claim.
- Do not use `official`, `authorized`, `exclusive`, or `certified partner` unless verified.
- Do not list hospital names as facts without approval.

**Safe default when unknown:** no partner claim at all.

## 10) Aesthetic services wording

- Use neutral, service-led wording.
- Focus on consultation, care, process, and support.
- Avoid promises about beauty, transformation, guaranteed confidence, or instant results.
- If a treatment is cosmetic, say so plainly without overclaiming benefits.

**Safe wording:** `aesthetic services`, `consultation-based treatment options`, `personalized review`.

## 11) Prohibited claims

Never publish:

- cure claims
- guaranteed results
- permanent results
- universal suitability
- risk-free claims
- before/after as proof of outcome
- unsupported hospital / partner claims
- unsupported licensing / regulator claims
- price or promo claims not approved by the client
- any claim that sounds like medical certainty without evidence

## 12) Allowed claims

Allowed when true and reviewed:

- service descriptions
- consultation-based wording
- educational explanations
- process summaries
- location / contact facts
- client-approved treatment categories
- final approved deliverables
- general, non-promissory benefits wording

**Good pattern:** `may help support`, `designed to`, `consultation-based`, `reviewed before delivery`.

## 13) Medical disclaimer rules

- Use a short disclaimer on medical or treatment pages when needed.
- Keep it plain and non-alarming.
- State that information is educational and does not replace clinician review.
- State that suitability and outcomes vary.
- Do not let a disclaimer compensate for unsafe claims.

**Working disclaimer:** `Information is for general education only and does not replace a medical consultation. Suitability and outcomes vary by person.`

## 14) Before / after policy notes

- Use only if client-approved and policy-compliant.
- Do not edit images in a misleading way.
- Do not imply one example is a guaranteed outcome.
- Keep consent, rights, and source notes attached.
- If uncertain, omit before/after from public copy and ads.

**Safe default when unknown:** no before/after material.

## 15) Google / Meta ad sensitivity

- Keep paid-ad copy conservative and educational.
- Avoid sensational weight-loss, body, or health-status wording.
- Avoid direct personal-attribute assumptions.
- Avoid before/after emphasis unless explicitly approved and compliant.
- Avoid unsupported medical or partner claims.

**Safe default:** consultation-led, neutral, and factual copy only.

## 16) BPOM / Indonesia sensitive topics

- Treat BPOM, permits, registrations, import status, and Indonesian compliance topics as verification-required.
- Do not state regulator approval or product compliance without current evidence.
- Keep Indonesia-specific medical language conservative.
- Escalate any claim that depends on a license, permit, or official status.

**Safe default when unknown:** omit the regulatory claim.

## 17) Target audiences

Do not use audience assumptions as facts unless the client confirms them.

## 22) Puriva operational data path

Use this path to move verified facts into working context without exposing raw intake notes to clients.

| Stage | Admin-owned input | Becomes | Never expose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intake | Goal, business context, target audience, offer context, location, notes | Brief notes and compliance notes | Raw intake dump, unverified claims, prompt text |
| AI Knowledge | Verified clinic profile, approved services, verified contact facts, approved disclaimers, verified audience / geography notes | Approved `AiKnowledgeItem` / `AiContextSnapshot` metadata | Raw notes, prompt injection, provider metadata |
| WorkflowBriefs | AI run results, MI/SEO reports, production plan, knowledge metadata | Brief detail, plan body, content-production seed state | Raw knowledge bodies, snapshot internals, client-visible leaks |
| SEO plan | Approved topics, taxonomy, priorities, verified claims, compliance status | Content plan objectives and draft requests | Final copy, public claims without review |
| AI Delivery | Content plan items, drafts, images, deliverables, WordPress draft handoff | Final archive and monthly report | Internal logs, `storageKey`, release-package internals, provider details |

Before SEO/content work starts, confirm:

- intake is complete;
- compliance review is documented in notes or plan body;
- approved knowledge/context exists for the verified facts;
- the AI Delivery project is linked or ready to link for downstream handoff.

If a fact is still pending verification, keep it in admin notes or WorkflowBriefs only. Do not promote it into client-facing copy, final reports, or approved knowledge.

Keep the bundle small and easy to verify.

## 23) Reporting expectations

Monthly reporting should clearly show:

- what was completed
- what was verified
- what is still pending
- what changed from the previous month
- what needs approval
- next-month recommendations

Reports should stay client-safe and avoid internal workflow detail.

## 24) Manual verification workflow

Use this order:

1. collect source evidence
2. compare each claim against the evidence
3. mark verified / unverified / needs review
4. route sensitive items to the approver
5. remove or soften anything unverified
6. record the reviewer and date
7. publish only client-safe final text

Evidence can be a client note, screenshot, document, URL, or signed approval.

## 25) Safe defaults when unknown

If a field is unknown:

- use `TBD`
- omit the claim
- mark it `needs verification`
- ask for evidence
- use generic client-safe wording
- do not guess

If a statement is sensitive:

- do not publish it until reviewed
- prefer neutral educational language
- use the least specific wording that is still true

If a page or deliverable is unverified:

- keep it internal
- do not place it in the client archive
- do not use it in ads or public SEO copy
