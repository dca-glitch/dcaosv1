# Client Wording Guide

Status: Plain-language guide for wording used with clients.

For Puriva, treat [`PURIVA_OPERATIONAL_INTAKE_AND_COMPLIANCE.md`](../runbooks/PURIVA_OPERATIONAL_INTAKE_AND_COMPLIANCE.md) as the approval source for any medical, service, partner, ad, or compliance wording.

This guide helps keep client communication simple, calm, and easy to understand.

## Main Rule

Clients should see clear completed work, not internal working notes.

Use language that explains outcomes, completed work, and next steps.

## Preferred Words

Use:

- final report;
- final deliverable;
- monthly summary;
- completed work;
- recommendations;
- next steps;
- prepared draft;
- publication handoff;
- client archive;
- approved content.

## Words To Avoid With Clients

Avoid:

- internal workflow;
- hidden context;
- debug;
- technical test;
- temporary proof;
- unfinished note;
- experimental work;
- system state;
- background process.

## Explaining AI Assistance

Good wording:

`We used AI-assisted research and drafting as part of our internal production process. All final work is reviewed before delivery.`

Better final-work wording:

`This content was reviewed and prepared for your monthly delivery.`

## Explaining Reports

Good wording:

`This report summarizes the work completed this month and the recommended focus for next month.`

For final client delivery wording:

`This final monthly report is read-only and ready for your archive.`

## Explaining Drafts

Good wording:

`A draft has been prepared for review.`

For final client-facing wording:

`The final article is ready for publication handoff.`

## Explaining Missing Metrics

Good wording:

`Live performance data is not available yet, so this report focuses on completed work and recommended next steps.`

## Explaining Client Area

Good wording:

`Your client area contains final approved work and reports.`

## Tone Rules

Client wording should be:

- clear;
- confident;
- short;
- practical;
- outcome-focused;
- free of technical implementation details.

## Monthly Update Tone

Use short paragraphs and plain next steps.

Lead with what was completed.

## Admin Reminder

If wording is about internal process, technical checks, hidden working notes, or unfinished work, it probably belongs in admin documentation, not client-facing material.
